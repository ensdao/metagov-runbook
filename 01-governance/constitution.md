# The ENS Constitution

The [Constitution](https://docs.ens.domains/dao/constitution/) binds all DAO governance. It has **five articles** and can only be changed by a **two-thirds majority with at least 1% of all tokens participating**.

| Article | Principle |
| --- | --- |
| I | Protection of name ownership. |
| II | Fees exist as an incentive mechanism, not for profit. |
| III | The treasury exists to ensure the long-term viability of ENS and fund its development; surplus funds go to public goods. |
| IV | ENS integrates with the wider DNS namespace. |
| V | Amendments: any change requires a two-thirds majority and at least 1% of all tokens participating. |

Article III is the normative basis for spending authorization — every treasury spend the Meta-Gov group oversees must serve ENS viability/development or public goods (see [treasury & endowment](../03-treasury-and-endowment/)). Amending the Constitution is a special [Constitutional proposal](proposals.md), submitted as a Snapshot social proposal with a diff.

## Verify note: the Constitution was never amended

The original ratified Constitution PDF — dated **08 November 2021**, "Signed by 84,350 accounts," in [ensdomains/governance-docs](https://github.com/ensdomains/governance-docs/blob/main/assets/Digital_ENS-DAO-Constitution.pdf) — already contained all five articles, **including Article V**. Article V was part of the original ratification, not a later addition. There has been **no amending proposal**, and the [live docs page](https://docs.ens.domains/dao/constitution) shows the same five articles unchanged. Any claim that the Constitution went from four to five articles is false.
