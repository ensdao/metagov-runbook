# DAO Structure & the Meta-Gov Mandate

The ENS DAO is organized around **Working Groups** run by elected **Stewards**, constrained by a five-article [Constitution](constitution.md), and operated through a multi-stage [proposal lifecycle](proposals.md). On-chain power flows ENS token → Governor → Timelock; see the [contract hierarchy](../02-contracts-and-multisigs/) and the canonical [address table](../02-contracts-and-multisigs/addresses.md).

## Current model (Term 7, 2026): single Working Group

For Term 7, a Snapshot ranked-choice (Copeland) vote, ["Path forward on Working Groups for Term 7"](https://discuss.ens.domains/t/path-forward-on-working-groups-for-term-7/22107), made **Meta-Governance the sole operational Working Group**, dissolving the Ecosystem and Public Goods WGs. Anyone running Meta-Gov today should treat the three-WG model as legacy. Term cycle and dates: see [elections.md](elections.md) ([nominations thread](https://discuss.ens.domains/t/meta-governance-working-group-steward-nominations-term-7-2026/22157)).

> ⚠️ **Open question:** Whether the Constitution or Working Group Rules pages were formally amended to reflect the single-WG structure, or whether it stands solely on the Snapshot outcome, is unverified.

## Legacy model (through Term 6 / 2025): three Working Groups

Historically there were three WGs (Meta-Governance, ENS Ecosystem, Public Goods), each with three elected stewards ([WG Rules](https://docs.ens.domains/dao/wg/rules/)). The Ecosystem and Public Goods WGs were [dissolved](https://discuss.ens.domains/t/path-forward-on-working-groups-for-term-7/22107) entering Term 7. For the full structural history (founding 4 WGs → Community dissolved → annual terms → single WG), see [05-terms/](../05-terms/README.md).

## Meta-Gov mandate

Meta-Governance handles **governance oversight and treasury/operational management** for the DAO ([Meta-Gov WG](https://basics.ensdao.org/metagovernance-wg)):

- Oversees DAO governance initiatives and tooling.
- Manages and supports working-group operations.
- Sets steward compensation standards before each nomination window; pays the Secretary.
- Administers funds allocated to it from the treasury.
- Holds the Endowment Manager (karpatkey) accountable under the Investment Policy Statement (see [treasury & endowment](../03-treasury-and-endowment/)).

Meta-Gov operates through a Working-Group Safe; for its seat model and live signing threshold see [stewards & roles](stewards-and-roles.md) and the canonical [address table](../02-contracts-and-multisigs/addresses.md).
